BULKANG TAAL
Bulkang Taal is a large, complex caldera located in the province of Batangas on the island of Luzon in the Philippines. It lies about 50 kilometers south of Manila. The volcano sits within Taal Lake, with all of its historical eruptions occurring on Volcano Island, an island located right in the middle of the lake. The caldera spans the municipalities of Talisay and San Nicolas. 		The tree(BGC)

Located in the province of Albay in the Bicol Region, Mayon Volcano is world-renowned for its perfectly symmetrical, conical shape. It is the most active volcano in the Philippines, with historical eruptions documented as far back as 1616. 		manila american cementy and memorial
Located in Taguig within Metro Manila's bustling Fort Bonifacio area, the Manila American Cemetery is the largest U.S. military cemetery overseas. Spanning 152 lush acres, it contains 17,206 graves and an impressive stone monument dedicated to over 36,000 missing service members. 		Fort santiago
a historic 16th-century citadel located at the mouth of the Pasig River in Intramuros, Manila. Built by the Spanish starting in 1571, it features a sprawling complex of moats, gardens, and fortified walls where you can explore the infamous dungeons and the Rizal Shrine honoring the country's national hero, José Rizal.

VISAYAS

White beach
The most famous is White Beach in Boracay, a renowned 4-kilometer stretch of powdery white sand and turquoise waters divided into three distinct stations. You can explore the highly-rated areas or check reviews on Tripadvisor and read a detailed layout guide at Philippine Travels. 		Biri island
Biri Island is a municipality in Northern Samar, Philippines, renowned for its seven monumental rock formations shaped by the colliding waters of the San Bernardino Strait and the Pacific Ocean. It requires traveling by land to Lavezares Port, followed by a 45-minute boat ride. 		The ruins
in Talisay City, Negros Occidental, is an iconic, Italianate mansion built in the early 1900s by sugar baron Don Mariano Ledesma Lacson. Dubbed the "Taj Mahal of the Philippines", it stands as a testament to tragic love and historical resilience. 		Kawasan Falls
is a renowned, three-tiered waterfall system located in the jungles of Badian, Cebu. Famous for its vibrant turquoise-blue, spring-fed waters, it features deep natural pools for swimming, bamboo rafting, and cliff jumping. The primary cascade drops about 40 meters into a lush, jungle-fringed canyon.What to Expect 		kalanggaman
Kalanggaman Island is a postcard-perfect tropical paradise in Palompon, Leyte, celebrated globally for its pristine powdery white sands and iconic, shifting sandbars. Measuring about 750 meters in length, the uninhabited island is famous for its crystalline turquoise waters, vibrant marine life, and tranquil, eco-tourism-focused environment.

MINDANAO

Siargao island
Siargao is a teardrop-shaped tropical island in the Philippine Sea. Known as the "Surfing Capital of the Philippines", it is famous for its legendary Cloud 9 waves, vast coconut forests, pristine white-sand beaches, and vibrant eco-tourism spots like Sugba Lagoon. 		Davao city
Davao City is a highly urbanized economic hub in Mindanao, Philippines, spanning over 2,400 square kilometers, making it one of the largest cities in the world by land area. Known as the "Durian Capital of the Philippines", it is celebrated for its lush landscapes, safe environment, and proximity to the country's highest peak, Mount Apo. 		Camiguin island
famously dubbed the "Island Born of Fire," is a pear-shaped volcanic island located in the Bohol Sea, just off the northern coast of Mindanao. Spanning only about 64 kilometers in circumference, it is highly accessible by air or ferry and features dramatic mountain peaks, pristine sandbars, hot/cold springs, and historic ruins. 		dahilayan adventure park
its a premier eco-adventure destination nestled at 4,500 feet above sea level in Manolo Fortich, Bukidnon. Famed for its cool, pine-scented climate, it features world-class extreme rides, dense forest trails, and family-friendly attractions, offering a thrilling escape in Northern Mindanao. 		the hinatuan enchanted river
in Surigao del Sur, Philippines, is a short, deep spring river famous for its crystal-clear sapphire waters and thriving underwater cave system. Legend credits its magical colors to fairies. For conservation, swimming is restricted to designated areas away from the deep main pool.
